Circuits

Everything great has to start somewhere.


General information
"This course is the first part of the education in circuit design, analysis and testing. In the lecture, the students will learn to apply circuit analysis techniques to solve basic electrical networks and to propose electrical network solutions to engineering problems. In the technical skill training the student will learn about basic electrical measurement equipment and circuits. The technical skill training is about writing technical reports."
(source: OSIRIS)

PRV:
  • PRV41: Reflection
Book(s) and other sources
  • Engineering Circuit Analysis (Irwin/Nelms, 11th edition)
This book contains explanations for circuit analysis techniques. It not a must-have, but it is convenient as it provides more than enough practice exercises. In addition, this book will be used again in the Networks part of Electronic Circuits 1.
  • Introducing Electronics
A PDF-file which contains practical information about circuit design for the accompanying TV-labs. Some chapters are obligatory for taking part in these lab sessions. Please refer to the study guide for these chapters.
Examination
  • Technical skills training (30%)
    • You will be graded every week on your preparation and your lab work.
  • Digital homework tests (20%)
    • These will test your knowledge of circuit analysis by giving you an example and asking to calculate certain values.
    • Most questions have three tries, and every wrong attempt will deduct points for that specific question.
  • Final exam (50%)
    • This exam will contain questions that are relevant with respect to the circuit analysis techniques that are taught in the lectures. The examples from the lectures are highly representative of the questions that'll be asked at this exam.
Contact hours
  • Lectures
    • 6 hours a week
    • The lectures will discuss circuit analysis techniques and show examples of how to apply these.
    • The six hours can roughly be divided into two parts
      • A block of 4 hours: In these lectures, new material is discussed.
      • A block of 2 hours: These lectures have the purpose of discussing examples.
  • Technical skills labs (TV-labs)
    • 4 hours a week
    • These lab sessions will teach you about how to build and test circuits.
    • Please note: Adequate preparation (refer to self-study for more information) for each session is obligatory.
    • During these labs, the PRV "Reflection" is assessed. You will have to hand in a written reflection upon what you did during the labs. Please refer to the study guide for more up-to-date information about this assessment.
Self-study
  • Re-doing exercises from the slides
    • This course has no list of mandatory exercises. The exercises from the lectures are roughly representative of the level of the questions at the exam. Re-doing some exercises from the slides might therefore be useful.
  • Exercises from the book
    • Even though no "lists" of exercises are given, it is worth spending some time solving problems from the book. The lectures follow the structure of the book, so finding a suitable exercise shouldn't be a problem.
  • Reading in the Introducing Electronics reader and preparing for the labs
    • The technical skills labs require from you that you adequately prepare for them. That involves reading relevant chapters from the reader, reading the lab exercises in advance, making notes of these and perhaps already drawing some schematics.
Tips
  1. The 2-hour lecture blocks are usually attended by a significantly smaller amount of students. That makes these hours perfect for asking questions about problems you've encountered.
