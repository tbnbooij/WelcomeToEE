Calculus B

It's great.


Book(s) and other sources
  • Calculus - a complete course (Adams/Essex, 8th edition)
This behemoth of a book is definitely worth picking up. It not only contains thousands of exercises and explanations, but is also used in the course Mathematics 1 (quartile 3).
  • Algebraic skills (EN)/Dictaat Rekenvaardigheden (NL)
This one is useful if you struggled with the starttest.
  • Lecture notes 'Vectors in plane and space' (NL/EN)
This PDF-file (available online) will teach you about vectors, dot products and cross products, which are part of this course's material.

  • Handout 'Notes on sets'
This handout contains basic mathematical notation that is used throughout the course.
  • Your own notes
Studying directly from the book is quite tough. Therefore, I highly advise you to take readable and extensive notes during the lectures. You'll thank yourself later for this, trust me.
Examination
  • 2WOB2: Starttest mathematical skills (10%)
    • Tests your initial knowledge of calculus.
    • Usually takes place on an evening.
  • 2WBB4: Weekly digital homework tests (10%)
    • The exercises may be infinitely redone until you get the full score, so please don't waste any points here.
  • 2WBB3: Intermediate test (10%)
    • Tests the material of the first 4 weeks.
  • 2WBB1: Final test (70%)
    • Consists of a set of multiple choice questions and a set of open questions.
Contact hours
  • Lectures
    • 6 hours a week
    • The lectures will discuss the material and a plethora of relevat examples.
    • I highly advise you to attend these and make extensive notes of what is being taught.
  • Tutor hour
    • 1 hour a week
    • During this hour, you can ask questions to a second-year student who passed this course. You may skip these, but if you're struggling with the material you might want to go.
Self-study
  • Exercises from the book
    • After every lecture, a list of exercises is given that help you better understand the taught concepts.
  • Tutor exercises
    • Some of the exercises mentioned above have to be worked out (the exercises of which the numbers are printed in boldface) and handed in, so that your tutor can check them and provide feedback.
    • These exercises will not graded and have no impact on your final grade.
  • Online exercises
    • OnCourse has multiple sets of exercises that have been sorted on subject.
    • Again, these exercises are not graded. They're merely used for self-study.
Tips
  1. Attend the lectures and take extensive notes. These will help you study a lot quicker for the exams, since studying from the book is nearly impossible (someone I know tried to do so, haven't heard from him ever since).
  2. It doesn't matter whether you use the 7th or the 8th edition of the book. The exercises are largely the same.
